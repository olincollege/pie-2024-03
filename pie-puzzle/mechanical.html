Mechanical Design Overview

Clock Puzzle

The first puzzle the user encounters is a clock. The user must move the minute hand to the correct time and the clock face will rotate counter-clockwise, revealing the next puzzle. The clock face is laser-cut with roman numerals rastered on. The hour and minute hands were 3D printed. On the underside of the minute hand is a magnet which triggers the clockâ€™s rotation when placed in the correct spot. The clock is attached to a 3D printed hexshaft attached to a servo that rotates the clock. Both the servo and hexshaft have mounts acting as supports to keep the clock from tipping over.

Key Box

The second puzzle the user encounters is the a latticed pie. On the pie there are several different lettered buttons to press. If the correct ones are pressed at the same time, a piece of the pie will pop out, revealing a small box containing the key to the prize. The pie is 3D printed, and underneath the correct buttons are spaces for switches that connect to the main circuit. Underneath the pie is a case which holds the solenoid that pushes the key box upward.

Integration/Housing

The main housing for our project is a cylindrical cage with a top plate that holds the clock and the lid, and a base plate holding the other puzzle components and electrical housing. The housing for the main circuit and other electrical components are underneath the second puzzle.

Around the clock is a pie tin made with cardboard and a 3D printed pie crust. There is white cloth draped below it to hide electrical components while still allowing us easy access. This helped increase the visual appeal of our puzzle box by making it look like a pie sitting on a table.

Once the user finds the key in the second puzzle, they can use it to open a locked wooden box made to hold the prize: pie.
